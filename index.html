Twopple is a 2D puzzle-platformer game created using Unity. The player must navigate through various levels and environments on a mission to get Twopple back home. Each level will have a variety of mechanics, but the core mechanic, drawing lines of ice, will remain vital throughout the game. This ability can be used to create platforms for Twopple, to prevent objects from moving, to direct objects already in motion, and many more functions the player will discover throughout the game.
DMKit is a website created to help DM Dungeons and Dragons. This tool not only streamlines the more cumbersome efforts that go into being a Dungeon Master, but it is also accessible regardless of the user's knowledge of the game. This website helps DMs manage six aspects of DMing: characters, monsters, NPCs, encounters, settlements, and quests. Made with the MERN (MongoDB, Express, React (with Redux), Node.js) stack, DMKit is a tool any DM would be elated to have in his or her toolbox. Click on the following link to visit the website: DMKit
Violocity is a mobile game where the player must maneuver around obstacles while the stage continuously gets faster until the player loses. To dodge the obstacles, the player must swipe to adjust the X and Y velocity of the yellow rectangle. The further the X and Y values get from 0, whether it be in the negative or positive direction, the more colorful the obstacles will become. The X velocity controls how blue the obstacles are, while the Y velocity controls how red they are. When the player has no velocity in either direction, the obstacles are black. If the velocity is maximum or minimum in both the X and Y directions, the obstacles will be a shade of violet. The game has two difficulties, with a steep increase in challenge when transitioning from \"Normal\" to \"Hard\". There are also powerups that players can get if they make it far enough in the level. If you want to check out Violocity on the Google Play Store, click here: Violocity
